# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Target User

**Exact title & stage**: VP Engineering or CTO at a Series A/B SaaS company (20–100 people)

**Why this persona**:
- They own the AI tool budget ($5k–$50k/month depending on team size)
- They have decision-making authority to change tools or negotiate with vendors
- They feel the pain of rising costs acutely (investors ask "why is AI spending so high?")
- Not engineers (they delegate day-to-day Cursor/Copilot). Not founders (they have other fires). VPs can allocate 15 min for an audit.

**Psychographics**:
- Paranoid about waste (Series A = every dollar matters)
- Competitive (other companies are probably spending less)
- Data-driven (need numbers, not vibes)

---

## Research Sources

**What they Google/read before wanting this tool**:
- "how much do startups spend on AI tools per developer" (annual benchmarking)
- "Cursor vs GitHub Copilot cost comparison" (tool evaluation)
- "is Claude Team worth it for small teams" (plan selection doubts)
- "AI tool spend optimization" (cost reduction)

**Signals they're ready to audit**:
- Recent tool adoption wave (just set up Cursor for the team)
- New CFO asking "why are we spending on 5 different tools?" (consolidation pressure)
- Invoice shock (monthly bill spiked, don't know why)
- Competitor talk (heard another startup cut AI spend 40%)

## Getting 100 Users in 30 Days (Zero Paid Budget)

### Phase 1: Week 1 (Launch Prep) — Target: 30 users
- **Show HN post** (well-written, honest about what the tool does and limitations)
  - Timing: Tuesday 6am PT (peak traffic)
  - Ask: "Does this help you audit your AI spend? What would make it more useful?"
  - Expected: 200–400 upvotes, 30–50 qualified visitors
  - Conversion (audit completion): 20–30%
  - **Outcome: 10–15 audits**

- **Twitter thread** (unroll demo, real audit examples)
  - Post: "I built a free tool that audited my team's AI spend. Found we were $2k/mo over budget."
  - Retweet from 2–3 founders with audiences
  - **Outcome: 10–15 audits**

- **Cold DMs on X** (to 20 CTOs running side projects, from earlier research)
  - Message: "Hey [name], I built a free AI spend audit tool. Took me 2 min, found $400/mo in savings. Thought you might find it useful."
  - Personal tone, no sales pitch, low friction
  - Expected response rate: 10–20%
  - **Outcome: 5–10 audits**

### Phase 2: Week 2–3 (Viral Loop) — Target: 40 users
- **Shared audits** (people share the `/audit/{uuid}` link on Slack/Twitter)
  - Clean OG preview ("saved $1,240/year" as the image preview)
  - Credex CTA in audit view for high-savings cases
  - **Outcome: 20–30 audits** (word-of-mouth amplification)

- **Indie Hacker Slack** (post in #launches + #funding, minimal pitch)
  - "Just shipped a free AI spend auditor, would love feedback"
  - Expected: 5 founders try it, 2–3 get real value
  - **Outcome: 5–10 audits**

### Phase 3: Week 4 (Momentum) — Target: 30 users
- **Dev.to blog post** ("Why We Built an AI Spend Auditor: 3 Surprising Findings")
  - Real data from Phase 1 audits (anonymized): average savings, most common overspend
  - SEO benefit (rank for "AI spend audit" in ~2 months)
  - Backlink from indie blog networks
  - **Outcome: 10–20 audits**

- **Reddit r/cto** (casual screenshot of the landing page, "what do you think?")
  - Low sales pressure (the product speaks for itself)
  - **Outcome: 5–10 audits**

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

**What only Credex (or a related team) can do:**

1. **Email existing Credex prospects** (if Credex has founder/CTO email list)
   - Subject: "Free audit: are you overpaying for AI tools?"
   - Offer: "See your specific savings + book a Credex consultation"
   - Conversion: 5–10% try the audit, 20–30% of those book a consult
   - **This is asymmetric advantage**: no other team has a warm audience of AI-spending-conscious CTOs

2. **Partner with AI tool communities** (Cursor Slack communities, Anthropic forums, etc.)
   - Post: "Audit your Cursor/Claude/ChatGPT spend against your team size"
   - These communities trust recommendations from actual users (not ads)
   - **Outcome: 20–50 users/week from each community**

3. **Embed in Credex onboarding flow**
   - New customers land on Credex → "want to see where you're overspending?" → SpendLens audit
   - Self-qualifying (customers are already thinking about AI spend optimization)
   - **Outcome: 50–100 users/month from internal funnel**

---

## Constraints & Assumptions

- **Cold outreach works better than paid ads** at this stage (specificity + authenticity > reach)
- **Show HN is still powerful** for B2B tools (CTOs read HN religiously)
- **People don't convert on trust alone**; they need to see real results (the audit itself is the conversion funnel)
- **Shared audits are the growth engine** — each shared audit is a free endorsement to a new audience

---

## Why This Beats "Do SEO"

- SEO takes 3–6 months to bear fruit (Show HN + Twitter work in days)
- Paid ads cost money (we're at zero budget)
- The viral loop (shared audits) is self-sustaining (marketing that compounds)
- Founder/CTO audiences are concentrated and reachable (not scattered across the web)

**Key metric to prove PMF**: Do people share their audit links? If viral coefficient > 1.0, we can scale.
